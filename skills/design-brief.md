# Design Brief Methodology

## When This Skill Activates
When Simon (hci-strategist) needs to sharpen strategic focus.

## The One Thing Test
If you cannot complete this sentence in under 15 words, your strategy is unfocused:
"This product helps [specific person] to [specific outcome] when [specific moment]."

## Exclusion Discipline
For every feature idea, ask:
1. Does this directly serve The One Thing?
2. If we remove this, does The One Thing still work?
3. If yes → exclude it. Write it in "What We Do NOT Build."

## Success Definition Framework
Bad: "Users are satisfied with the app" (unmeasurable, vague)
Good: "A user who opens the app at 11pm feeling anxious closes it at 11:12pm feeling ready to sleep" (specific, time-bound, emotional state change)

Template: "[User] who [before state] → after using this product → [after state] within [timeframe]"

## Non-Negotiable Identification
Ask: "If we had to cut everything to ship in one week, what is the ONE thing we would keep?"
That is the non-negotiable.

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] The One Thing is truly one thing (no "and")
- [ ] What We Build describes an experience, not a feature list
- [ ] What We Do NOT Build has at least 3 items with reasons
- [ ] Success Definition describes user state change, not metrics
- [ ] Non-Negotiable is something concrete that could actually be cut under pressure
